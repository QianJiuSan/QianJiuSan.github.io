The nomal equations

Gradient descent gives one way of minimizing J. Let’s discuss a second way of doing so, this time performing the minimization explicitly and without resorting to an iterative algorithms. In this method, we will minimize J by explicitly taking its derivatives with respect to the θ[j]’s, and setting them to zero. To enable us to do this without having to write reams of algebra and pages full of matrices of derivatives, let’s introduce some notation for doing calculus with matrices.
梯度下降给了一种求最小 J 值的方法。现在我们来讨论第二种，这次不采取重复计算的算法，用能准确的完成最小值的算法。这个理论里，我们将通过明确的对 θ[j] 求导来使 J 最小化，并使它们约等于 0。为了让我们不用计算大量的代数问题和通篇的矩阵派生物，我们定义一些关于矩阵演算的提示。

To make our matrix notation more concrete, let us now explain in detail the meaning of the first of these equations. Suppose we have some fixed matrix B∈R^(nm). We can then define a function f: R^([m] [n])–> R according to f(A)=tr AB.
